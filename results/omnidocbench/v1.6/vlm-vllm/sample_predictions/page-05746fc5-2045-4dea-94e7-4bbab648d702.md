Theorem 5.4. Let \(a, b\) be freely independent positive random variables in an ncps \((\mathcal{A}, \varphi)\). Let \(\mu\) and respectively \(\nu\) be the distributions of \(a\) and \(b\) with respect to \(\varphi\), and assume that neither of \(\mu, \nu\) is a point mass. Then \(d\mu_{a,a^{1/2}ba^{1/2}}^{(\mathrm{ov})}(s,t) = \mathrm{o}_{a,a^{1/2}ba^{1/2}}(s,t)\mu(ds)\mu \boxtimes \nu(dt)\). In particular \(\mu_{a,a^{1/2}ba^{1/2}}^{(\mathrm{ov})} \ll \mu \times \mu \boxtimes \nu\) and \(\mathrm{o}_{a,a^{1/2}ba^{1/2}}\) is the overlap function of \(a\) and \(a^{1/2}ba^{1/2}\).

The proof of this theorem is very similar to the one of Theorem 4.4.

Corollary 5.5. With the notation of Theorem 5.4, for every \( f \in \mathrm{Bor}_b(\operatorname{Spec}(a)) \) we have

\[
E [ f (a) \mid a ^ {1 / 2} b a ^ {1 / 2} ] = h (a ^ {1 / 2} b a ^ {1 / 2}),
\]

where \(h:\mathrm{Spec}(a^{1 / 2}ba^{1 / 2})\to \mathbb{C}\) is defined (in \(\mu \boxtimes \nu\) -almost everywhere sense) by

\[
h (t) = \left\{ \begin{array}{l l} - \frac {1}{\pi t f _ {\mu} \boxtimes_ {\nu} (t)} \int_ {\mathbb {R} _ {+}} f (s) \operatorname{Im} \left(\frac {1}{1 - \omega (1 / t) s}\right) d \mu (s) & \quad \text {if} t \in \mathrm{supp} (\mu \boxtimes \nu) ^ {a c} \setminus E, \\ f \left(\frac {1}{\omega (1 / t)}\right) & \quad \text {if} t > 0 \text {is an atom of} \mu \boxtimes \nu , \\ \frac {1}{\nu (\{0 \})} \int_ {\mathbb {R} _ {+}} \frac {f (s)}{1 - s \psi_ {\mu} ^ {- 1} (\nu (\{0 \}) - 1)} d \mu (s) & \quad \text {if} t = 0 \text {and} \mu (\{0 \}) <   \nu (\{0 \}), \\ f (0) & \quad \text {if} t = 0 \text {and} \mu (\{0 \}) \geq \nu (\{0 \}). \end{array} \right.
\]

Here again, the proof is exactly the same as in the additive case.

We will calculate the conditional expectation in the case \( f(s) = s \) and then we will further specialize \( b \) to be a free Poisson element, in which case we will retrieve the well-known Ledoit-Péché estimator of covariance matrix from [21]. The calculations are non-trivial only in two cases: \( t = 0 \) when \( \mu(\{0\}) < \nu(\{0\}) \) and when \( t \in \mathrm{supp}(\mu \boxtimes \nu)^{ac} \setminus E \).
