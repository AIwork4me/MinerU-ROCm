G.1.3. BOUNDS ON THE SECOND MOMENT ALONG THE FINITE-PARTICLE SYSTEMS

In this section, we show that along the finite-particle dynamics (16) and algorithm (20), the distributions remain in \(\mathcal{P}(\mathbb{R}^{2dN})\).

Under Assumption 1, recall from Theorem 35 (in Section H) that there exists a unique equilibrium point \( z^{*} = (x^{*}, y^{*}) \in \mathbb{R}^{2d} \) which satisfies \( \nabla V(z^{*}) = 0 \). Define \( \mathbf{z}^{*} = (x^{*}, \ldots, x^{*}, y^{*}, \ldots, y^{*}) \in \mathbb{R}^{2dN} \). Then by construction, \( b^{\mathbf{Z}}(\mathbf{z}^{*}) = 0 \), where \( b^{\mathbf{Z}} \) is the vector field defined in (17).

Lemma 31 Assume Assumption 1. Suppose \(\mathbf{Z}_t \sim \rho_t^{\mathbf{Z}}\) evolves following the finite-particle dynamics (16) in \(\mathbb{R}^{2dN}\) from \(\mathbf{Z}_0 \sim \rho_0^{\mathbf{Z}} \in \mathcal{P}(\mathbb{R}^{2dN})\). Then \(\rho_t^{\mathbf{Z}} \in \mathcal{P}(\mathbb{R}^{2dN})\) for all \(t \geq 0\).

Proof We note that \(\rho_t^{\mathbf{Z}}\) is absolutely continuous with respect to the Lebesgue measure on \(\mathbb{R}^{2dN}\) by virtue of the Brownian motion component in the dynamics (16). We now show that the second moment of \(\rho_t^{\mathbf{Z}}\) remains finite for all \(t\geq 0\). Since \(\mathbf{Z}_t\) evolves following the dynamics (16), its density \(\rho_t^{\mathbf{Z}}\) evolves following the Fokker-Planck equation:

\[
\frac {\partial \rho_ {t} ^ {\mathbf {Z}}}{\partial t} = - \nabla \cdot \left(\rho_ {t} ^ {\mathbf {Z}} b ^ {\mathbf {Z}}\right) + \tau \Delta \rho_ {t} ^ {\mathbf {Z}}.
\]

From this, we can compute, using integration by parts:

\[
\begin{array}{l} \frac {d}{d t} \mathbb {E} _ {\rho_ {t} ^ {\mathbf {Z}}} \left[ \| \mathbf {Z} _ {t} - \mathbf {z} ^ {*} \| ^ {2} \right] = \int_ {\mathbb {R} ^ {2 d N}} \frac {\partial \rho_ {t} ^ {\mathbf {Z}} (\mathbf {z})}{\partial t} \| \mathbf {z} - \mathbf {z} ^ {*} \| ^ {2} d \mathbf {z} \\ = \int_ {\mathbb {R} ^ {2 d N}} \left(- \nabla \cdot \left(\rho_ {t} ^ {\mathbf {Z}} b ^ {\mathbf {Z}}\right) (\mathbf {z}) + \tau \Delta \rho_ {t} ^ {\mathbf {Z}} (\mathbf {z})\right) \| \mathbf {z} - \mathbf {z} ^ {*} \| ^ {2} d \mathbf {z} \\ = \int_ {\mathbb {R} ^ {2 d N}} \rho_ {t} ^ {\mathbf {Z}} (\mathbf {z}) \left\langle b ^ {\mathbf {Z}} (\mathbf {z}), \nabla \left(\| \mathbf {z} - \mathbf {z} ^ {*} \| ^ {2}\right) \right\rangle d \mathbf {z} + \tau \int_ {\mathbb {R} ^ {2 d N}} \rho_ {t} ^ {\mathbf {Z}} (\mathbf {z}) \Delta \left(\| \mathbf {z} - \mathbf {z} ^ {*} \| ^ {2}\right) d \mathbf {z} \\ = 2 \int_ {\mathbb {R} ^ {2 d N}} \rho_ {t} ^ {\mathbf {Z}} (\mathbf {z}) \left\langle b ^ {\mathbf {Z}} (\mathbf {z}), \mathbf {z} - \mathbf {z} ^ {*} \right\rangle d \mathbf {z} + \tau \int_ {\mathbb {R} ^ {2 d N}} \rho_ {t} ^ {\mathbf {Z}} (\mathbf {z}) (4 d N) d \mathbf {z} \\ = 2 \int_ {\mathbb {R} ^ {2 d N}} \rho_ {t} ^ {\mathbf {Z}} (\mathbf {z}) \left\langle b ^ {\mathbf {Z}} (\mathbf {z}) - b ^ {\mathbf {Z}} \left(\mathbf {z} ^ {*}\right), \mathbf {z} - \mathbf {z} ^ {*} \right\rangle d \mathbf {z} + 4 \tau d N \\ \leq - 2 \alpha \int_ {\mathbb {R} ^ {2 d N}} \rho_ {t} ^ {\mathbf {Z}} (\mathbf {z}) \| \mathbf {z} - \mathbf {z} ^ {*} \| ^ {2} d \mathbf {z} + 4 \tau d N \\ = - 2 \alpha \mathbb {E} _ {\rho_ {t} ^ {\mathbf {Z}}} \left[ \left\| \mathbf {Z} _ {t} - \mathbf {z} ^ {*} \right\| ^ {2} \right] + 4 \tau d N \\ \end{array}
\]

where the inequality above uses the property that  \( -b^{Z} \)  is  \( \alpha \) -strongly monotone, from Lemma 12. We can write the differential inequality above equivalently as:

\[
\frac {d}{d t} \left(e ^ {2 \alpha t} \mathbb {E} _ {\rho_ {t} ^ {\mathbf {Z}}} \left[ \| \mathbf {Z} _ {t} - \mathbf {z} ^ {*} \| ^ {2} \right]\right) \leq e ^ {2 \alpha t} 4 \tau d N.
\]

Integrating from 0 to t and rearranging the result gives:

\[
\mathbb {E} _ {\rho_ {t} ^ {\mathbf {Z}}} \left[ \| \mathbf {Z} _ {t} - \mathbf {z} ^ {*} \| ^ {2} \right] \leq e ^ {- 2 \alpha t} \mathbb {E} _ {\rho_ {0} ^ {\mathbf {Z}}} \left[ \| \mathbf {Z} _ {0} - \mathbf {z} ^ {*} \| ^ {2} \right] + \frac {(1 - e ^ {- 2 \alpha t})}{\alpha} 2 \tau d N
\]

\[
\leq \mathbb {E} _ {\rho_ {0} ^ {\mathbf {Z}}} \left[ \left\| \mathbf {Z} _ {0} - \mathbf {z} ^ {*} \right\| ^ {2} \right] + \frac {2 \tau d N}{\alpha}.
\]
